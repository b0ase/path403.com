## Definition: Interface as Logic
**Interface as Logic** is the principle that UI components (buttons, forms, transitions) are functional operators in a business process, not just visual wrappers. A button that is hard to find is functionally equivalent to a backend endpoint that returns a 404.

---

## The Fallacy of "Look and Feel"

Engineers often treat Design as "paint"â€”a layer applied after the "real work" is done. This is a fatal error.

In specific technical terms:
*   **Latency is Logic**: A slow transition breaks the causal link in the user's mind.
*   **Clarity is Validation**: A confusing form input is a validation error before the data is even sent.
*   **Aesthetics are Trust**: A broken layout signals a broken security model to the amygdala.

## The ROI of Excellence

We don't design for "delight." We design for **flow**.
Flow is the state where the user's intent maps 1:1 to system action without cognitive friction.

## Our Approach
